Life Story

As you already know, my name is Michael Newby and was born and raised in Harlem, NY. Ya boy grew up with a twin brother and a slightly younger sister. The first school that I went to was The Red Balloon School in Harlem for pre-k. This is where he met my life-long friend. The next year, I moved on to go to P.S. 87 on the upper west side, where I unconciously started to discover what I was interested in, performing and entertaining. I found myself drawing a lot on my own, and with a couple friends, and playing instruments. It eventually became something I was really good at. In the 4th grade, I switched schools to attend George Jackson Academy, a start-up all boys private school, which obviously I wasn't excited about. There I got into quiet a bit of touble with some other kids (but a room full of black people from the ghetto... what would you expect?) and stopped drawing. But I continued to progress in music After a couple years, I grew to learn from my mistakes and managed to stay out of trouble in school until I graduated. After graduating, I went on to attend the Trevor Day School. It was a MAJOR culture shock for me, but it was also where I discovered my love of track. Although, there are a lot of differences between me and the other students in the PWI, I found a small group of people who I had a lot in common with and they became my core group of friends.
